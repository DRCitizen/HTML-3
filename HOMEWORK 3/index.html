The Soundtack in Mafia II game

The Mafia II Soundtrack features 130 songs by their original artists. The music is divided into two eras, with separate playlists for 1945 and 1951, the periods covered in gameplay. Music may be listened to on radios inside vehicles and buildings and select songs are used in cutscenes.While the bulk of the tracks played in the 50s era are anachronistic, having come out well after the 1951 time frame set by the game, the soundtrack is still noted as being one of the largest collections of licensed songs ever assembled in a video game, with each one considered classic selections in their genre.

Empire Bay Radio Stations

Empire Bay Radio Stations consist of Delta Radio, Empire Central Radio, and Empire Classic Radio. There is also Press Radio News, a service that broadcasts news about World War II on all three stations in 1945. The stations can be listened to while driving or inside a building on a radio. The music and genres on each station changes as the era changes.

Empire Central Radio

Empire Central Radio is one of the three radio stations in Empire Bay. Empire Central Radio, or ECNR, is hosted by Richard Foxberry in the 1940s, being replaced by "Rockin" Ricky Fox in the 1950s. The station mainly plays big band music during the 1940s segment and more mainstream pop and rock during the 1950s portion.

Delta Radio

Delta Radio is one of the three radio stations in Empire Bay. Delta Radio, or EDLT, is hosted by Walt Williams in the 1940s, being replaced by Sammy Stevens in the 1950s. The station mostly plays jazz and blues music during the 1940s segment, and doo-wop and rock-and-roll hits during the 1950s portion.

Empire Classic Radio

Empire Classic Radio is one of the three radio stations in Empire Bay. Empire Classic Radio, or ECLA, is hosted by Bob Berriman in the 1940s. He is replaced as host by Gary Stevens in the 1950s, who covers breaking news while Bob Berriman continues to report on general news stories. The station's headquarters can be found in Midtown. The station plays big band and swing music in both segments of the game but introduces more popular contemporary tracks in the 1950s.
